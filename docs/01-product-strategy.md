# Housemate Product Strategy

Last updated: 2026-04-22

## What Housemate Is

Housemate is an AI-powered home management platform: a digital superintendent for homeowners. The product combines an always-on SMS agent with a web dashboard that helps a household stay on top of vendors, maintenance, records, reminders, and follow-through.

## Core Category Definition

Housemate is:

- an AI property manager for homeowners
- a trust product built around memory, judgment, and follow-through
- a homeowner-first platform with vendor monetization as a secondary layer

Housemate is not:

- a marketplace-first business
- a generic life assistant today
- a smart-home hardware platform
- a warranty replacement or insurance product

## Customer Truth

The customer is the homeowner or household. Vendor-side features exist to improve homeowner outcomes and support revenue, but homeowner trust wins every tie.

Primary residential segments:

- Established household: busy, high-income, asset-rich, time-poor
- New homeowner: overwhelmed by the transition from renter to owner
- Independent senior: needs coordination support to preserve independence

## Problem Statement

Running a home creates ongoing cognitive load: scheduling service, tracking what was done, managing vendors, remembering maintenance, coordinating payments, and knowing what matters now. Current tools are too fragmented, too reactive, or too dependent on the homeowner staying organized.

Housemate exists to remove that coordination burden.

## Product Promise

The promise is not "AI for your home." The promise is:

`The level of home care that used to require a staff.`

Users should feel:

- nothing important is falling through the cracks
- someone competent is holding the thread
- they do not have to remember everything themselves

## Strategic Positioning

- Web-first, SMS-primary
- Homeowner-first, vendor revenue ancillary
- Calm premium service standard, not a utilitarian home-maintenance app
- Quality of execution matters more than breadth of features

## Revenue Model

- Pro subscription: `$299/year` or `$34/month`
- 30-day free trial, no credit card required to start
- Annual plan should be the default commercial emphasis
- Vendor placement and intent data are secondary revenue layers
- Portfolio / commercial tier is a later expansion, not a v1 concern

## Strategic Moat

The moat is not the model alone. It is the combination of:

- compounding household data
- delegated access infrastructure
- trusted homeowner relationship
- local vendor density and history
- closed-loop operational memory

## Guardrails

- Never optimize vendor monetization at the expense of homeowner trust.
- Do not let paid placement distort recommendations.
- Do not broaden into general life management before winning in home.
- Do not confuse a polished UI with proven product-market fit.

## Geographic Approach

- Market 1: Atlanta suburbs
- Market 2: Charlotte
- Markets 3+: selected through a scoring model after real usage data exists

## What Must Be True Before The Product Is Working

Housemate is working when early users reliably feel:

- "It remembered that for me."
- "It followed through."
- "It handled that without me having to babysit it."

If the product merely looks polished or conceptually impressive, it is not yet working.
